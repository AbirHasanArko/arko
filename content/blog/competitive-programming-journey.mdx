## Why competitive programming

I started CP the way most people do: out of curiosity, then out of frustration, then out of habit.
The first 20 problems were painful. The next 50 were illuminating. By 100, I'd internalized patterns
I now use every day at work — sliding windows, prefix sums, binary search on the answer.

## What CP actually teaches

- **Decomposition** — break a fuzzy problem into smaller, well-defined steps.
- **Edge cases** — you'll never write a clean `if` again without thinking about bounds.
- **Communication** — explaining a solution clearly is a superpower.

## Teaching at SGIPC

I took instructor sessions for SGIPC, KUET's competitive programming circle. The biggest lesson:
**don't show solutions**. Show *intuition*. The "aha" moment is what students remember.

## Recommended path

1. Solve 30 easy problems on Codeforces.
2. Read the editorial even if you solved it — there's always a cleaner way.
3. Compete in virtual contests. Pressure reveals gaps.
4. Teach someone. If you can't explain it, you don't know it yet.

The 100+ problems I solved in 2024 changed how I write production code more than any tutorial I read.
